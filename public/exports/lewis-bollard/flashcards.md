## (00:02:18) — Opening

### Q1. Why is factory-farmed chicken such a brutal price target for alternative proteins or higher-welfare substitutes?

Chicken is close to `2 × cheap grain + minimal overhead` economics.

The bird efficiently converts grain into meat, and factory farming removes many costly welfare inputs like space and comfort.

## (00:12:14) — 00:09:54 – Tech that’s sparing 100s of millions of animals/year

### Q1. What is in-ovo sexing and what does it replace?

The egg industry has no use for male chicks: they cannot lay eggs and are not efficient meat birds. So they are usually killed after hatching.

In-ovo sexing scans eggs early, identifies male embryos, and removes those eggs before they hatch. 

### Q2. Give an example of the factory-farming “patch cycle” Bollard points to, where one welfare problem leads to another cruel intervention.

Example: pigs are brought indoors and bred for faster growth/aggression -> they bite tails -> tails are cut off -> teeth are clipped -> sows are put in crates -> drugs are added. Each patch manages a symptom while leaving the underlying system intact.

## (00:17:02) — 00:16:16 – Brainless chickens and higher welfare breeds

### Q1. Why doesn’t animal suffering automatically get selected away in factory farming if sick or injured animals are less productive?

If cramming twice as many animals into a barn causes 10% more deaths, the farm can still produce more meat overall.
